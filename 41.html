1.
I AM TO v
.
In the past and present tense, there is short form to this pattern. It is not used in a negative sentence,
positive question or negative question. I AM SUPPOSED TO is used much more.
(present)
He is to go there today.
=
He is supposed to go there today.
I am to talk to him later.
=
I am supposed to talk to him later.
(past)
They were to see him today.
=
They were supposed to see him today.
She was to buy it there.
=
She was supposed to be buy it there.

.
This is another variation.
(past)

When was she due to get here?
(present)
Is she due to get the prize?
What is she due to give him?
