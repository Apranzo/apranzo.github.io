These variations of MORE THAN are always in a negative sentence or question.
1.
ANY implies that the two things being compared are about the same.
It isn't any bigger than this one.
(It is about the same size.)
She didn't study any more than me.
(We studied about the same time.)
2.
MUCH implies the first thing is little better, smaller etc. in a sentence. In a question, it implies "a lot ".
He wasn't much taller than her.
(He is a little taller.)
Didn't they eat much more than you?
(They ate much more than you.)
